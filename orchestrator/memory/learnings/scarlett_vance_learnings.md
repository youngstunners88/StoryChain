## Mistakes Log

### [Seeded] Resolving tension instead of deepening it

**What happened:** Two characters are circling each other. Instead of tightening the tension, the segment has one of them simply say what they feel. The score drops. The story flattens.

**Why it happened:** The impulse to give characters what they want. But in romance, withholding is the engine.

**Pattern to avoid:** Do not let characters name their desire in the first half of the story.

**Better approach:** Let them do everything *except* say it. The reader should be screaming internally. That is the target state.

---

### [Seeded] Physical description that reads like a catalogue

**What happened:** A character is described with a series of physical attributes — hair, eyes, jaw, shoulders. Quality gate detects low interiority, low tension.

**Why it happened:** The convention of romance has standardised physical description. But cataloguing is not attraction — it is inventory.

**Pattern to avoid:** No more than one physical detail per encounter. Make that detail specific and unexpected.

**Better approach:** One physical detail that reveals something about the observer's psychology. Not what they look like — what I notice about how they look.

---

### [Seeded] Obstacles that are misunderstandings rather than genuine incompatibilities

**What happened:** The only thing keeping two people apart is information one of them doesn't have. When readers can see how easily it would resolve, engagement drops.

**Why it happened:** Misunderstanding is the easiest obstacle. It requires no character depth.

**Pattern to avoid:** The obstacle must emerge from who these specific people are, not from what they don't know.

**Better approach:** Real incompatibilities. Real wounds. Real fears. The thing that keeps them apart should still be present even after all secrets are revealed.

---

## Patterns to Avoid (Summary)

| Pattern | Rule |
|---------|------|
| Premature declaration | Desire stays unspoken until it cannot |
| Physical inventory | One unexpected detail beats five standard ones |
| Misunderstanding obstacle | The barrier must be a character flaw, not missing information |
| Even-paced feeling | Romance needs rhythm: advance, retreat, advance further |
| Neat resolution | Love is not solved — it is chosen, over and over |

---

## Better Approaches

- **Austen principle:** The best romantic tension is social. The obstacle is propriety, expectation, class — forces larger than the individuals.
- **Rooney principle:** Love in the modern era is anxious and inarticulate. Characters want but cannot ask for what they want.
- **Craft rule:** Every scene between two romantic leads must end with more tension than it started with.
