# Section 1: Email Automation Prompts

## Prompt 1: Customer Welcome Sequence
```
You are an expert email copywriter. Write a 5-email welcome sequence for a new customer who just signed up for [PRODUCT/SERVICE].

For each email provide:
- Subject line (under 50 characters)
- Preview text (under 100 characters)
- Email body (150-200 words)
- CTA button text

Tone: Warm, helpful, not salesy

Email sequence:
1. Welcome + What to expect
2. Quick start guide
3. Tips for best results
4. Success story/case study
5. Check-in + Next steps
```

## Prompt 2: Sales Follow-Up Emails
```
Write a follow-up email sequence for a prospect who showed interest in [PRODUCT/SERVICE] but hasn't purchased.

Context:
- They [downloaded our guide / attended webinar / requested demo]
- It's been [X days] since last contact
- Our product costs [PRICE]
- Main benefits: [BENEFIT 1, BENEFIT 2, BENEFIT 3]

Write 3 follow-up emails:
1. Value-add email (share a relevant tip/resource)
2. Case study email (show results others achieved)
3. Urgency email (limited time offer or bonus)

Each email under 200 words. Include subject lines.
```

# Bonus: Prompt Engineering Tips

## Getting Better Results

1. **Be Specific**: The more context you provide, the better the output
2. **Set Constraints**: Word counts, tone, format help focus responses
3. **Provide Examples**: Show what you want when possible
4. **Iterate**: Use follow-up prompts to refine
5. **Break Down Complex Tasks**: Multiple focused prompts work better
