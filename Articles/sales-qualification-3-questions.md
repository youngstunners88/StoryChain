## The 3 Questions

Most salespeople waste weeks on leads that were never going to buy. Three questions in the first three minutes tells you if this is worth your time:

1. **What are they using now?**
2. **What's broken about it?**
3. **What does it cost them if it stays broken?**

If they can't answer the third one, there's no urgency. Move on.

## The Principle

Qualifying isn't about being difficult. It's about being efficient with your time and theirs.

## Related Framework: SPIN Selling

One commenter noted this maps to SPIN selling:
- **S**ituation - What are they using now?
- **P**roblem - What's broken about it?
- **I**mplication - What does it cost them if it stays broken?
- **N**eed-Payoff - Benefits of your solution (the missing fourth piece)

## Key Insights from Comments

- "Second ask works well. They'll inevitably tell you what's not working."
- "The CoI (Cost of Inaction) is the real art"
- "Never quote your price before the client has quantified their problem out loud"
