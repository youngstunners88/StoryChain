# Boober GDPR Compliance Document

**Version 1.0 | February 2025**

## Overview

While Boober primarily operates in South Africa and complies with POPIA (Protection of Personal Information Act), we recognise that some users may be EU/EEA residents or our operations may involve processing data of EU citizens. This document outlines our GDPR compliance approach for such circumstances.

## Applicability

GDPR applies to Boober when:
- Processing personal data of EU/EEA residents
- Offering goods/services to individuals in the EU/EEA
- Monitoring behaviour of individuals in the EU/EEA

| Processing Activity | Legal Basis | Justification |

| Ride Processing | Contract | Necessary for service delivery |
| Payment Processing | Contract | Necessary for payment completion |

## Data Subject Rights

Boober facilitates the following GDPR rights:

### 1. Right to Access (Article 15)
- Users can request a copy of their personal data
- Provided within 30 days via the App or email
- Free of charge

### 2. Right to Rectification (Article 16)
- Users can correct inaccurate data via App settings
- Manual corrections via support request

### 3. Right to Erasure (Article 17)
- Users can delete their account via App settings
- Data deleted within 30 days (subject to legal retention requirements)

### 4. Right to Restrict Processing (Article 18)
- Users can request processing restrictions
- Evaluated case-by-case

### 5. Right to Data Portability (Article 20)
- Users can export their data in JSON format
- Available via App settings

### 6. Right to Object (Article 21)
- Users can opt out of marketing communications
- Users can object to profiling for direct marketing

### 7. Rights Related to Automated Decision-Making (Article 22)
- Fare calculations are automated but not solely automated decisions
- Users can request human review of significant automated decisions

## Privacy by Design

Boober implements Privacy by Design principles:

### 1. Proactive not Reactive
- Privacy impact assessments before new features
- Regular privacy audits
- Security-first development practices

### 2. Privacy as the Default
- Data minimisation in all data collection
- Opt-in for marketing and non-essential features
- Automatic data deletion after retention periods

### 3. Privacy Embedded into Design
- Privacy considerations in all development sprints
- Code review includes privacy checks
- Architecture designed for data protection

### 4. Full Functionality
- Privacy measures don't compromise functionality
- User experience designed with privacy in mind

### 5. End-to-End Security
- Encryption throughout data lifecycle
- Secure data transmission and storage
- Regular security assessments

### 6. Visibility and Transparency
- Clear privacy policy
- Transparent data practices
- User-accessible privacy controls

### 7. Respect for User Privacy
- User-centric privacy controls
- Easy-to-use privacy settings
- Clear communication about data use

## Data Protection Impact Assessments (DPIA)

DPIAs are conducted for:
- New features involving personal data
- Changes to data processing activities
- New third-party integrations
- Cross-border data transfers

### DPIA Process:
1. Identify need for DPIA
2. Describe processing and purposes
3. Assess necessity and proportionality
4. Identify and assess risks
5. Identify measures to mitigate risks
6. Document and review

## Data Breach Notification

Under GDPR, Boober commits to:
- Notify supervisory authority within 72 hours of becoming aware of a breach
- Notify affected data subjects without undue delay if high risk
- Document all breaches and remediation actions

## Records of Processing Activities

Boober maintains records of all processing activities including:
- Purposes of processing
- Categories of data subjects and personal data
- Categories of recipients
- Transfers to third countries
- Retention periods
- Security measures

## International Data Transfers

When transferring data outside the EU/EEA:

### Approved Mechanisms:
- Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs)
- Binding Corporate Rules (where applicable)
- Explicit consent for specific transfers

### Current Transfer Routes:
| Data Recipient | Location | Mechanism |
|----------------|----------|-----------|
| AWS (Primary) | South Africa | Adequacy consideration |
| Payment Processor | USA | SCCs |
| Analytics Provider | USA | Anonymised data only |

## Data Protection Officer

Boober has appointed a Data Protection Officer:
- Contact: dpo@boober.co.za

## Supervisory Authority

EU/EEA residents may lodge complaints with their local supervisory authority. For cross-border issues, the lead supervisory authority is determined by Boober's main establishment in the EU (if applicable).

## Employee Training

All staff with access to personal data receive:
- GDPR awareness training upon hiring
- Annual refresher training
- Role-specific privacy training
- Phishing and security awareness training

## Vendor Management

Third-party processors are selected based on:
- GDPR compliance certification
- Data protection agreement signing
- Security certifications (ISO 27001, SOC 2)
- Regular compliance audits

## Compliance Monitoring

Regular compliance activities:
- Monthly privacy metrics review
- Quarterly internal audits
- Annual external audit
- Continuous monitoring of regulatory changes
