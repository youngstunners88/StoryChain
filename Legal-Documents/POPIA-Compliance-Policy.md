## 1. Introduction

### 1.1 Purpose
This POPIA Compliance Policy ("Policy") establishes the framework for the lawful processing of personal information in accordance with the Protection of Personal Information Act 4 of 2013 ("POPIA"), South Africa's comprehensive data protection legislation.

### 1.2 Scope
This Policy applies to:
- All employees, contractors, and temporary workers
- All processing of personal information by or on behalf of the Company
- All systems, applications, and processes that handle personal information

### 1.3 Legal Framework
POPIA gives effect to the constitutional right to privacy while balancing this right against other rights, particularly the right of access to information. This Policy ensures compliance with POPIA's eight conditions for lawful processing.

## 2. Definitions

In addition to definitions in POPIA, the following definitions apply:

- **"Company"** means [Company Name], registration number [Registration Number].

- **"Information Officer"** means the person designated in terms of section 55 of POPIA.
- **"Operator"** means a person who processes personal information on behalf of the responsible party.
- **"Processing"** means any operation or activity concerning personal information, including collection, use, disclosure, and deletion.
- **"Responsible Party"** means the public or private body that determines the purpose and means of processing personal information.

## 3. Conditions for Lawful Processing

POPIA establishes eight conditions for the lawful processing of personal information. The Company adheres to each condition as follows:

### 3.1 Condition 1: Accountability

The Company, as the responsible party, remains accountable for compliance with POPIA even when processing is outsourced to operators. We ensure that:
- Operators are bound by contract to establish appropriate security safeguards
- Operators assist the Company in responding to data subject requests
- Regular audits are conducted to verify operator compliance

### 3.2 Condition 2: Processing Limitation

Personal information is processed in a manner that is:
- **Lawful:** Processing has a legal basis under POPIA
- **Minimised:** Only necessary personal information is collected
- **With Consent:** Where required, explicit consent is obtained
- **Collected from Data Subject:** Information is collected directly from the data subject unless an exception applies

### 3.3 Condition 3: Purpose Specification

Personal information is collected only for a:
- Specific purpose
- Explicitly defined purpose
- Lawful purpose related to the Company's functions

The purpose for collection is documented and communicated to data subjects before or at the time of collection.

### 3.4 Condition 4: Further Processing Limitation

Further processing of personal information must be:
- Compatible with the purpose for which it was initially collected
- Authorized by the data subject
- Permitted by law

### 3.5 Condition 5: Information Quality

The Company takes reasonable steps to ensure that personal information is:
- Complete
- Accurate
- Not misleading
- Updated where necessary

Data subjects may request correction of inaccurate personal information.

### 3.6 Condition 6: Openness

The Company maintains transparency by:
- Documenting all processing operations
- Making processing information available upon request
- Providing clear privacy notices to data subjects

### 3.7 Condition 7: Security Safeguards

The Company implements appropriate, reasonable technical and organisational measures to secure personal information, including:

**Technical Measures:**
- Encryption of personal information at rest and in transit
- Access controls and authentication mechanisms
- Regular security updates and patches
- Firewalls and intrusion detection systems
- Secure backup procedures

**Organisational Measures:**
- Written security policies and procedures
- Access restrictions on a need-to-know basis
- Staff training on data protection
- Regular risk assessments
- Incident response procedures

### 3.8 Condition 8: Data Subject Participation

Data subjects have the right to:
- Request confirmation of whether their personal information is being processed
- Request access to their personal information
- Request correction, deletion, or destruction of personal information
- Lodge a complaint with the Information Regulator

## 5. Data Subject Rights

### 5.1 Right of Access
Data subjects may request access to their personal information. Requests must be processed within 30 days, subject to POPIA's requirements.

### 5.2 Right to Correction
Data subjects may request correction, deletion, or destruction of inaccurate personal information.

### 5.3 Right to Object
Data subjects may object to the processing of their personal information on reasonable grounds relating to their particular situation.

### 5.4 Right to Lodge a Complaint

## 6. Processing of Special Personal Information

Special personal information includes:
- Religious or philosophical beliefs
- Race or ethnic origin
- Trade union membership
- Political persuasion
- Health or sex life
- Biometric information
- Criminal behaviour

Processing of special personal information is prohibited unless:
- The data subject consents explicitly
- Necessary for establishment of a right in law
- Required for medical purposes
- Authorized by law

---

## 7. Transborder Information Flows

Personal information may only be transferred to a third party in a foreign country if:
- The recipient is subject to a law or binding agreement providing an adequate level of protection
- The data subject consents to the transfer
- The transfer is necessary for the performance of a contract
- The transfer is for the benefit of the data subject and obtaining consent is impracticable

---

## 8. Data Breach Management

### 8.1 Incident Response
In the event of a suspected or confirmed data breach:
1. The incident is immediately reported to the Information Officer
2. An investigation is initiated to determine the scope and impact
3. Containment measures are implemented

### 8.2 Notification Obligations
The Company notifies:
- The Information Regulator (as soon as reasonably possible)
- Affected data subjects (unless the data is encrypted or similar safeguards apply)

Notifications include:
- Description of the breach
- Identity of the person who may have accessed the data
- Consequences of the breach
- Measures taken to address the breach

---

## 9. Retention and Destruction

### 9.1 Retention Periods
Personal information is retained only for as long as necessary to achieve the purpose for which it was collected, unless:
- Retention is required by law
- Retention is required for a contract
- The data subject has consented to longer retention

### 9.2 Destruction and Deletion
Personal information is securely destroyed or deleted when no longer required, using methods appropriate to the medium:
- Paper documents: Secure shredding
- Electronic records: Secure deletion or physical destruction of storage media
