# Boober Marshal Guidelines

**Confidential - For Internal Use Only**

This document provides sensitive operational guidelines for Boober marshals working in the South African taxi industry. These procedures are designed to ensure smooth integration with existing taxi operations while maintaining safety and efficiency.

---

## 1. Understanding the Taxi Industry Context

### 1.1 Industry Landscape

South Africa's taxi industry is a critical component of public transportation:
- Over 250,000 minibus taxis operating nationally
- Taxi associations hold significant influence at local ranks
- Rank management traditionally handled by associations
- Strong community ties and cultural significance

### 1.2 Our Approach

Boober works **with** the taxi industry, not against it. Our marshals are integrated into existing structures rather than replacing them.

**Core Principles:**
- Respect existing rank hierarchies
- Build relationships with association leadership
- Complement rather than disrupt traditional operations
- Ensure fair treatment of all drivers

## 2. Rank Capacity Reporting

### 2.1 Reporting Process

**Step-by-Step:**
1. Open the Marshal App
2. Navigate to "Rank Status"
3. Enter the following data:

| Field | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| Queue Length | Number of taxis waiting |
| Estimated Wait | Minutes for next taxi |
| Demand Level | Low / Medium / High / Critical |
| Conditions | Weather, events, road closures |

4. Add any relevant notes
5. Submit - data updates in real-time

### 2.2 Capacity Indicators

Use these guidelines to assess rank capacity:

**Low (Green)**
- 0-3 taxis in queue
- Wait time: < 5 minutes
- Action: Minimal reporting needed

**Medium (Yellow)**
- 4-8 taxis in queue
- Wait time: 5-15 minutes
- Action: Standard reporting

**High (Orange)**
- 9-15 taxis in queue
- Wait time: 15-30 minutes
- Action: Increase reporting frequency

**Critical (Red)**
- 15+ taxis in queue
- Wait time: 30+ minutes
- Action: Immediate report; consider passenger diversion

### 2.3 Special Situations

**Rush Hours (6-9 AM, 4-7 PM)**
- Report every 20 minutes
- Anticipate high demand
- Coordinate with association leaders

**Weekends and Holidays**
- Monitor religious gatherings
- Report event-related surges
- Adjust for market closures

**Weather Events**
- Report road conditions
- Note taxi availability impact
- Communicate delays to passengers

## 3. Safety Protocols

### 3.1 Personal Safety

**Do:**
- Wear visible Boober identification at all times
- Stay in populated, well-lit areas
- Keep your phone charged and accessible
- Know emergency exit routes from the rank
- Trust your instincts - leave if threatened

**Don't:**
- Carry large amounts of cash
- Confront aggressive individuals physically
- Leave valuables visible in your vehicle
- Share your home address with drivers
- Accept rides from strangers

### 3.2 Rank Safety Standards

**Physical Hazards to Report:**
- Broken lighting
- Potholes or uneven surfaces
- Blocked emergency exits
- Unauthorized vendors
- Suspicious packages

**Reporting Process:**
1. Photograph the hazard (if safe)
2. Submit through Marshal App
3. Alert rank management
4. Follow up if not resolved

### 3.3 Medical Emergencies

**Immediate Actions:**
1. Call emergency services (10111 / 10177)
2. Do not move injured persons unless danger is imminent
3. Keep the area clear
4. Document the incident
5. Report to Boober immediately

## 4. Conflict Resolution

### 4.1 Common Conflicts

**Queue Disputes**
- Listen to all parties
- Review arrival order (ticket system if available)
- Apply rank rules consistently
- Escalate to association leader if needed

**Passenger-Driver Issues**
- Mediate calmly and professionally
- Focus on facts, not emotions
- Offer practical solutions
- Document serious incidents

**Association Conflicts**
- Remain neutral
- Do not take sides
- Report to Boober management
- Allow leadership to resolve

### 4.2 Escalation Protocol

**Level 1 - Self Resolution**
- Apply de-escalation techniques
- Use rank procedures
- Time limit: 10 minutes

**Level 2 - Marshal Supervisor**
- Contact shift supervisor
- Provide detailed incident report
- Time limit: 15 minutes

**Level 3 - Security/Police**
- Call security or police
- Ensure personal safety
- Document everything after
- File complete incident report

### 4.3 De-escalation Techniques

1. **Active Listening**
   - Give full attention
   - Don't interrupt
   - Nod and acknowledge

2. **Validation**
   - "I understand why you're frustrated"
   - "That is a valid concern"

3. **Finding Common Ground**
   - "We both want smooth operations"
   - "Let's find a solution"

4. **Clear Communication**
   - Use simple language
   - Avoid technical terms
   - Stay calm

5. **Offering Choices**
   - Present 2-3 options
   - Allow them to choose
   - Follow through on commitments

## 5. Working with Taxi Associations

### 5.1 Key Relationships

**Association Leadership:**
- Meet local chairpersons and secretaries
- Understand their concerns
- Report relationship issues immediately

**Rank Owners/Operators:**
- Identify who manages the rank
- Understand their rules
- Coordinate schedules

**Veteran Drivers:**
- Learn from experienced drivers
- Understand informal hierarchies
- Build trust over time

### 5.2 Communication Guidelines

**DO:**
- Greet everyone respectfully
- Use appropriate titles (Mr., Ms., Chairperson)
- Ask how you can help
- Report concerns promptly
- Follow through on commitments

**DON'T:**
- Make promises you can't keep
- Criticize existing procedures
- Show favouritism
- Discuss association politics
- Make deals without authority

### 5.3 Sensitive Issues

**Addressing Concerns:**
1. Listen without defensiveness
2. Acknowledge their perspective
3. Explain Boober's position clearly
4. Offer to escalate to management
5. Follow up within 24 hours

**Common Concerns:**

| Concern | Response |
|---------|----------|
| Loss of income | Boober brings additional passengers |
| Disruption of queue | We integrate with existing systems |
| Unfair competition | All drivers can join Boober |
| Data privacy | We protect driver information |
| Control of rank | We support, not replace, management |

### 5.4 Building Trust

**Short-term Actions:**
- Be present and consistent
- Learn names quickly
- Help where appropriate
- Stay neutral

**Long-term Actions:**
- Deliver on promises
- Report accurately
- Respect traditions
- Communicate regularly

## 6. Operational Procedures

### 6.1 Daily Schedule

**Pre-Shift (30 min before):**
- Check in via app
- Review any notices
- Coordinate with previous marshal

**Shift Operations:**
- Manage queue
- Assist passengers
- Report capacity
- Handle issues

**Post-Shift (15 min after):**
- Complete shift report
- Handover to next marshal
- Submit any incidents

### 6.2 Reporting Requirements

**Routine Reports:**
- Shift summary (end of each shift)
- Capacity updates (every 30 min minimum)
- Equipment issues

**Immediate Reports:**
- Safety incidents
- Major disputes
- Police involvement
- Medical emergencies

**Weekly Reports:**
- Trend analysis
- Suggestions
- Relationship updates

### 6.3 Equipment

**Required:**
- Boober Marshal App (updated)
- Fully charged phone
- Marshal identification vest
- Data connectivity

**Recommended:**
- Portable charger
- Water bottle
- Rain jacket
- Basic first aid

## 7. Legal and Compliance

### 7.1 Relevant Laws

- National Land Transport Act, 2009
- Road Traffic Act
- Consumer Protection Act (for passenger disputes)
- POPIA (data protection)

### 7.2 Your Authority

**You CAN:**
- Manage queue order
- Report capacity
- Assist passengers
- Mediate minor disputes
- Escalate issues

**You CANNOT:**
- Arrest or detain people
- Physically remove people
- Confiscate property
- Make legal decisions
- Commit Boober to agreements

### 7.3 Documentation

Always document:
- Incident details (who, what, when, where)
- Witnesses
- Actions taken
- Outcomes

This protects you and Boober.
